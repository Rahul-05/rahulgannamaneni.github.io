Avicii responsive music website

Overview

PASSION PROJECT • UX DESIGN • Responsive web design

Designing a website for a music artist to present his work to gain more user attention and sell merch while identifying the lack of user mass in current music sites.

Roles & Responsibilities

• User Research: Surveys, Competitive Analysis, Interviews, User testing, Affinity Mapping
• UX Design: sketches, Iterative Wireframing, low and high fidelity prototyping, Testing Sessions

Project Context

• Solo Project
• Oct 2021 -1 week

Tools Used

• Adobe xd
• Miro

The Problem

For a music artist merch is a big part of his or her revenue. But as observed within previous data there is so less surge for a music website from the user's side.

This leads to low sales in merch. Merch is not just for revenue it indirectly gives a kind of advertisement to brand and artist, so it's necessary that people frequently visit an artist's website to make his brand appear all over the place.

Current music websites only narrowed towards merch but people need more of purpose to the site to frequently visit the site.

Process

User research

1. I conducted interviews with 10 invidual and created empathy maps to understand the users.
2. Primary user groups were identified through research Was users with high intrest in music and visits conserts frequently.
3. This user group confirmed initial assumptions about the music websites, but research also revealed users visits music sites less frequently because its uses are so narrowed towards only merch.
4. Users complained mostly about how sites are not most relevant with the brand identity of the artist there are fond and that make them feel little.
4. Others resources mentioned that music sites don't particularly have the best online store purchase experience.

User group-1

• User group-1 is defined as Users who frequently visit concerts or frequent merch buyers.
• These user groups are highly interested in specific artists and visit their music sites for merch but they won't visit the site frequently because they don't feel the brand identity in the site and music sites lack consistency.

User group-2

• User group-2 is defined as users who are popular with music genres and constantly listen to music and rarely buy merch.
• These user groups even though they like the artist, new merch updates never reach them because they don't visit the website frequently because there is no other purpose they find for a music site.

Research Insights

Lack of visits to music sites

Music sites are mstly narrowed towards merch they but as the products are so narrowed towards only sigular brand, so users have no reason to visit site frequently.

Mising brand identity in music sites

Mostly every music site is filled with a background of the current album is current album but already existing buttons and details don't support every color identity so the site loses that brand identity touch and also levels up with new accessibility issues.

Missing Intuitive User-flow

Research popped up results that point to a lack of good user flow for music sites. Users' main target through these sites is the purchase of merch but it's not suitable and most convenient for most users.

Feasible solutions

Giving other purposes to music sites

• Embedding music sites with new purposes will allow users to visit the website.
• Adding features like track and album listening and also updates on new songs with quick links to concert tickets to allow users to get better engagement with the site.

Consistency of brand identity through out website

The common problem with using new background photos and colors is accessibility and missing brand identity but if we use consistent brand colors throughout the site. we can also solve this issue by using an overlay that matches the brand colors this will overcome users' issues.

Intuitive User-flow

After comparing music sites with popular sites online shopping sites like amazon new details have come into top changes. The users just need consistency with user flow and the user interface they are passing through to get a better experience.

Overview

• Landing page has been designed so users would feel the brand identity of the artist and will observe a unique detail.
• Landing page is filled with quick links that will redirect the user to New releases, Features, and Tour showcases so that the user has a short user flow when just wanting to overview the website.




As users only visit artist websites only for shopping music gives them a reason to visit the website and scroll through and interact with other necessary features. This will allow users to interact with the artist's brand more frequently and it helps the artist to get more user attention to grow in his field.




Shopping experience.

• Users can quickly cart any item without overviewing the full product. This helps when the user already knows the product and can edit his decisions in the cart.
• Users can review and check others' opinions on the product just below the overview of the product so that they can get a better understanding of the product.




Your orders.

Your orders sections can be visited by users to get knowledge on present order situation or previous order details.

Respnsive Overview

Design has been adapted to multiple screens using responsive web design concepts. Responsive web design was made within the continuity and consistency of desktop screen to make users more familiar across the devices.

As per research insights, I enabled the Space app with easy device switching. now users switch from parent device to child device or vice-versa in settings pannel using use as an option.




Music listening flow.

With the gradual decrease in screen size, a relevant number of details have been downgraded like the number of plays per song and album details so that the limited details get more attention and users don't feel overwhelmed by the extra details in small screens size.




Store flow.

User flow for shopping has kept to limited with necessary details within each step and gaining towards necessary details by the end of purchase.

Next steps

We need to address details with KPIs after the launch of the website and get details from existing users to level up more user issues for a more diverse group.
