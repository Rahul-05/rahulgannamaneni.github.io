Guide: Evolving from “Netflix for Training” to a Seamless Content Platform for Mental Health

"It all started with one question: How can we make it easier to find the right training content? But as we dug deeper, a bigger story unfolded."

The Challenge: A Dual-Phase Problem Statement

The Initial Problem & Context

Guide started as a corporate LMS where companies uploaded 90-second training videos for employee development. However, the platform lacked a dedicated search system—users were forced to manually browse through endless content.

"Guide did not have an efficient search system. Employees spent too much time manually browsing through content, which hampered engagement and slowed down learning."

Guiding Questions:

How might we create a search interface that enables users to quickly find relevant training content?

How can we integrate personalized recommendations that streamline the learning journey?

The Pivot: Evolving the Problem Statement

As our project progressed, Guide pivoted its focus. Instead of solely serving corporate training, the platform began targeting mental health—where therapists (as content providers) and patients (as users) replaced companies and employees.

"Users now need an efficient way to find therapeutic content, while providers require tools to assign, track, and manage treatment progress."

Guiding Questions:

How might we refine the search system to address the unique needs of mental health content?

How do we design a two-sided interface that serves both patients and therapists seamlessly?

Research Phase 1: Building the Foundation

Business Model Canvas (BMC)

We began by mapping out the entire business model to understand key value propositions, target users, revenue streams, and partnerships.

  • Key Insights: The freemium model, community building, and the “bottom-up” sales approach emerged as critical components.
  • Focus Areas: Content producers, user engagement, and seamless guest access.

Secondary Research

We conducted a deep dive into academic papers and books on search patterns, best practices, and user experience in online learning environments.

  • Focus: Indexing methods, controlled vocabularies, relevance algorithms, and mobile-friendly design.
  • Outcome: A list of best practices that would serve as a benchmark for our search design.

Competitive Analysis

We analyzed leading platforms such as Cornerstone OnDemand, LinkedIn Learning, Absorb LMS, TalentLMS, Skilljar, and Xensai.

  • Key Features Identified: Personalized recommendations ,Predictive search and autocomplete ,Microlearning previews and faceted search
  • Takeaway: Competitors were effectively using these features to drive engagement; our goal was to adapt and innovate for Guide’s context.

User & Expert Interviews

We conducted interviews with 3 business owners from diverse industries (startups to established companies) to understand how they discover training content.

  • Core Questions:
  • How do you prioritize search results (relevance, length, quality)?
  • Would personalized recommendations influence your search behavior?
  • What features are most valuable in a search experience?

Insights:

Compliance Focus:

Training was often seen as a checkbox task.

Curated Recommendations:

Users preferred systems that suggested relevant content.

Content Previews:

The ability to preview content was highly valued.

The Pivot & Research Phase 2: Re-Aligning for Mental Health

Following our initial rounds, Guide pivoted its business model toward mental health content for therapists and patients. This required a new round of rapid research:

  1. Updated Interviews: Conducted a one-week crunch of additional user and expert interviews (3 healthcare professionals and 1 expert from Guide) to understand the new user needs.
  2. New Problem Statement: "Patients need an efficient way to find therapeutic content, while providers require robust tools to assign, track, and manage treatment adherence." <
  3. Competitive Feature Analysis: Revisited competitors in the mental health and telehealth space (e.g., Talkspace, Amwell) to inform our updated design.

Next Steps

Our work has led us to a critical juncture. With initial ideation, mid-fidelity prototyping, and a subsequent research pivot complete, we now have a clear direction for refining our high-fidelity prototypes and further testing.

  • Ideation: Brainstorm new features tailored to mental health content, including advanced progress tracking and a two-sided interface for patients and providers.
  • High-Fidelity Prototyping: Convert our updated designs into polished prototypes.
  • User Testing: Run further usability tests with healthcare professionals and patients.

Final Reflection Quote:

"This project taught us that the real power of design lies in its ability to adapt—transforming challenges into opportunities, one iteration at a time."
