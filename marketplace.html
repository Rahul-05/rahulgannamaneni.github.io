Contribution

I was the sole designer on this project, owning the entire process from initial research to the final interactive prototype.

I worked closely with product managers, security experts, and HR stakeholders. Their insights helped refine every aspect of the design.

The Challenge: From a Simple Question to an Expanding Scope

When I first joined Keka HR as a User Experience Design Intern in April 2023, I was tasked with one specific challenge: simplify the Azure AD integration process. Initially, the goal was narrowly defined—reduce complexity in an already convoluted system. However, as I began my research, a broader story emerged.

I quickly discovered that what appeared to be a simple integration problem was, in fact, a symptom of deeper issues:

  • Rigid Setup: The existing process forced users into a one-size-fits-all configuration.
  • Lack of Transparency: There was no clear indication of whether integrations were active or secure.
  • Limited Control: HR teams had no quick way to toggle integrations based on real-time needs.

Problem Statements

1. How might we simplify the AD integration process while accommodating diverse user needs?

2. How can we provide real-time visibility and immediate control to empower HR teams and system administrators?

Quick Look at the Final Solution

Our redesigned solution features a unified dashboard with real-time integration statuses, a guided customization flow, and intuitive one-click toggle controls.

Results at a Glance:

1. Unified Dashboard: Displays real-time statuses with clear, color-coded cards and dynamic icons.

2. Instant Controls: One-click toggles empower rapid activation or deactivation.

3. Guided Customization: Step-by-step process that simplifies integration setup.

Impact: 40% increase in user satisfaction, 25% reduction in management time, and 85% of users now feel more secure.

Diving Into Research

I began with direct interviews and rapid in-house usability tests with HR professionals and system administrators. Key findings included:

  • Customization Overload: Users were forced into a one-size-fits-all setup.
  • Opacity in Status: There was no clear indication of whether an integration was active or secure.
  • Limited Control: HR teams lacked immediate ways to toggle integrations as needed.

User Quote:

"I often end up chasing updates instead of managing our integrations proactively," noted one system admin during testing.

Secondary research from industry articles and academic papers reinforced these findings, emphasizing centralized dashboards and intuitive controls.

"User-centric interfaces drive efficiency and enhance trust," as noted in a leading UX research journal.

Feature Analysis: Learning from the Competition

I also performed a detailed feature analysis of comparable enterprise systems. This review highlighted what our competitors were doing right:

  • Centralized Dashboards: Offering real-time status updates.
  • Guided Onboarding Flows: Reducing cognitive load by breaking complex processes into manageable steps.
  • Instant Control Features: Such as one-click toggles for quick action.

Transforming Insights into Design:

Mapping the Journey & Ideation

I began by charting the existing user journey, identifying key friction points from initial setup to daily management. This exercise illuminated exactly where users were getting stuck.

"Every friction point in the journey was a clue, each one drove us to a more intuitive design."

Design: Sketching, Wireframing, & Iteration

Armed with insights, I translated my ideas into low-fidelity sketches and wireframes. The early prototypes focused on three main components:

  1. Centralized Dashboard: A unified view displaying real-time statuses with color-coded cards and dynamic icons.
    Design Note: Introduced color-coded cards and dynamic icons for clarity. "Status Card – Green: Active, Red: Error."
  2. Guided Customization Flow: A step-by-step process that simplifies the integration setup.
    Design Note: "Simplified language and progressive disclosure were used to reduce cognitive overload."
  3. Quick Toggle Controls: Intuitive one-click toggles for rapid activation or deactivation.
    Design Note: "These controls were designed to be prominent yet seamlessly integrated within the dashboard."

Rapid Prototyping & User Testing

I advanced from paper sketches to interactive prototypes. Despite resource constraints, rapid usability testing sessions with our in-house HR team provided invaluable feedback:

  • Dashboard Clarity: Users immediately grasped the value of a consolidated view.
  • Guided Flow Efficiency: The step-by-step approach dramatically reduced configuration errors.
  • Toggle Usability: The intuitive toggle controls were well received.

"The new dashboard cuts through the clutter—it’s so much easier to see what needs attention."

The Final Design: A Visual & Measurable Transformation

The final design reflects a balanced blend of functionality and simplicity:

  • Unified Dashboard: Presents real-time integration statuses in a clean, intuitive layout.
  • Guided Setup Flow: Walks users through each configuration step, making complex tasks manageable.
  • Instant Toggle Controls: Empowers users with immediate, on-demand management of integrations.

Measurable Outcomes:
– User Satisfaction: Increased by 40% in follow-up surveys.
– Time Efficiency: 25% reduction in time managing integrations.
– Security Confidence: 85% of users now feel more secure.

Reflections & Future Directions

"Every design decision was a step toward building a more intuitive, secure, and user-friendly experience."

This project evolved from a single question to a comprehensive redesign that redefined the integration experience at Keka HR. Through direct user feedback, extensive primary and secondary research, and rapid iterative testing, we transformed a rigid, opaque process into an intuitive solution that meets real-world needs.

  • User-Centered Insights: Direct feedback and secondary research guided every decision.
  • Iterative Design: Rapid prototyping and continuous testing drove significant improvements despite resource limitations.
  • Visual Storytelling: Annotated images and clear comparisons communicated our design evolution effectively.
